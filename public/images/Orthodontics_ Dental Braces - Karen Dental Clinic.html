We understand that each patient has unique needs and preferences when it comes to orthodontic treatment. Thats why we offer a variety of options to suit different lifestyles, budgets, and treatment goals:

Traditional Metal Braces

The time-tested solution that remains highly effective for correcting even complex alignment issues. Todays metal braces are smaller and more comfortable than ever before.

Ceramic Braces

Featuring clear or tooth-colored brackets that blend with your natural tooth color, these provide a more discreet treatment option while offering the same effectiveness as metal braces.

Self-Ligating Braces

Advanced brackets that dont require elastic ties, resulting in less friction, potentially shorter treatment times, and fewer adjustment appointments.

Clear Aligners

Transparent, removable trays that gradually shift teeth into position. Perfect for patients seeking a nearly invisible treatment option that can be removed for eating and cleaning.

Lingual Braces

Custom-made braces attached to the back surfaces of your teeth, making them completely hidden from viewideal for adults concerned about appearance during treatment.

There are many questions about the service, we have selected frequently asked questions about this service. If you do not see your answer, please contact us.

How long will my orthodontic treatment take?
Treatment time varies based on individual factors including the complexity of your case, the type of correction needed, and the treatment method chosen. On average, comprehensive orthodontic treatment ranges from 12-24 months. Simple alignment cases with clear aligners might be completed in as little as 6 months, while more complex bite corrections might take up to 30 months. During your consultation, we'll provide a personalized estimate based on your specific situation, as well as milestone targets so you can track your progress.
Do braces or orthodontic treatment hurt?
While you may experience some discomfort, particularly in the first few days after braces are placed or adjusted, modern orthodontics is far more comfortable than in the past. Today's braces use lighter forces and more comfortable materials. Most patients describe the sensation as pressure rather than pain, which typically subsides within 3-5 days and is easily managed with over-the-counter pain relievers. Clear aligners generally cause even less discomfort than traditional braces.
